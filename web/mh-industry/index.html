Do more-industrial districts have healthier children?

Across — Maharashtra districts: industrial intensity (x) against child stunting (y, NFHS-5). One dot per district — the shape of the cloud answers the question honestly, without pretending 35 districts are a nation.

Districts

How to read it. A downward-sloping cloud means more-industrial districts tend to have less child stunting; a shapeless cloud means no relationship. The line is a simple linear fit; r is the correlation (−1…+1). This is descriptive, not causal, and not graded good/bad.
