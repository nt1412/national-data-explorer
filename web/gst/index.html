Which states produce — and which consume

GST is a destination tax, so the IGST settlement reveals it: a state's post-settlement SGST (what accrues after goods made elsewhere are consumed there) vs its pre-settlement SGST (tax on its own output). High ratio = net consumer; low = net producer. FY 2024-25.

Read it right. This is descriptive economic geography, not a league table — colour is the producer↔consumer axis, not good/bad. Remittance geography ≠ where activity happened: large firms register at their HQ, so Maharashtra, Karnataka and Delhi are over-credited on own-SGST relative to where production or consumption physically occurred. That is the single biggest caveat.
